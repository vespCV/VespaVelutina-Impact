- **Sampling sessions**: 138 sessions (199 h 23 min total)
- **Sessions <90 min**: 14 sessions (due to weather or disturbance)

### Landscape Influence
- **Foraging area composition** (2 km radius around nests):
  - Forest and semi-natural areas (CLC 3): 48.35%
  - Agricultural areas (CLC 2): 41.24%
  - Artificial surfaces (CLC 1): 9.90%
  - Water bodies (CLC 5): 0.51%
- **Prey collection**: Mainly in field and forest areas
- **Correspondence analysis**: Distinguished prey spectrum of colonies in fields/forests vs urban/wet areas
- **Urban/wet areas**: More Apis mellifera, Mecoptera, Tachinidae, other Hymenoptera
- **Forest/field areas**: More social wasps, meat-flies

### Temporal Dynamics
- **Peak predation**: Early October (around day 277, ordinal date)
- **Peak time of day**: Around midday
- **Model**: Quadratic effects of dates and hours (GLMM)
- **Apis mellifera proportion**: Significantly decreased during season (best model: date and hour, linear and quadratic)
- **Vespidae proportion**: Increased early season, reduced late season
- **Diptera proportion**: Higher early (July) and late (November), lower during peak activity
- **Prey diversity**: Similar trend to predation activity (peak early October)

### Impact on Honeybees
- **Comparison**: Average colony consumes as many bees as produced by single hive
- **Hive production estimate**: 110,000 bees (June-October: 10k, 20k, 30k, 25k, 25k respectively)
- **Proportion**: ~40% of individuals produced by one hive could be consumed by one hornet colony
- **Wild bees**: Only 0.02% of prey (bumblebees and solitary bees, excluding wild honeybee colonies)

## Other Quantitative Data

### Environmental Conditions
- **Climate type**: Atlantic climate
- **2020 mean external temperature**: 13.5 °C
- **2021 mean external temperature**: 12.9 °C
- **2020 mean external relative humidity**: 92.4%
- **2021 mean external relative humidity**: 91.9%
- **2020 mean precipitation**: 4.1 L/m²
- **2021 mean precipitation**: 5.5 L/m²
- **Temperature range for hornet activity**: 5-33 °C
- **Relative humidity range for hornet activity**: 40-100%
- **Optimal temperature for hornet activity**: 15-25 °C
- **Optimal relative humidity for hornet activity**: >60%

### Hornet Monitoring
- **First sighting 2020**: 12 June 2020
- **First sighting 2021**: 26 May 2021
- **Beekeeper first notice 2020**: July (1 month after camera detection)
- **Beekeeper first notice 2021**: July (1.5 months after camera detection)
- **Monitoring system**: Photo monitoring (APEMAN 20MP 1080P Hunting Camera)
- **Camera specifications**: 940nm IR Brightness LED, IP66 Waterproof
- **Camera distance**: ~1 m from hives
- **Photo window area**: 200×150 cm²
- **Photo frequency**: Every hour from 6:00 h to 0:00 h
- **Total photographs analyzed**: 9,567 photographs
- **Data points analyzed**: 94,000 data points per sensor

### Hornet Activity Patterns
- **Daily activity period**: 7:00 h - 21:00 h
- **Peak months 2020**: September (maximum number of hornets)
- **Peak months 2021**: October (exponential increase)
- **July-August pattern**: Non-central hours (10:00-13:00 and 16:00-20:00)
- **September-October pattern**: Central hours of the day (mid-day)
- **Temperature avoidance period**: 13:00-16:00 h (maximum temperatures, minimum relative humidity)

### Correlations
- **Hornet sightings vs external temperature**: r = 0.368, p < 0.01 (positive correlation)
- **Hornet sightings vs external relative humidity**: r = -0.347, p < 0.01 (inverse correlation)
- **Correlation strength by month**: Lower in July-August (bimodal distribution), higher in June, September, October

### Colony Strength Measurements
- **Measurement method**: Modified protocol by Delaplane et al. (2017)
- **Measurement frequency**: Twice per year per colony
- **Measurement parameters**: Brood, bee population, food reserves (calculated as percentages)

#### Colony 1 (C1) - 2020
- **March**: Swarmed (decrease in population)
- **Winter brood**: 9%
- **Winter bee population**: 50%
- **Winter food reserves**: 23%

#### Colony 2 (C2) - 2020
- **Winter brood**: 14%
- **Winter bee population**: 60%
- **Winter food reserves**: 19%

#### Spring 2021
- **C1 brood**: 35%
- **C2 brood**: 33%
- **C1 food reserves**: 36%
- **C2 food reserves**: 39%
- **No significant differences** between colonies

#### Summer 2021
- **C2**: Lost population, died at end of study

### Thermoregulation
- **Internal temperature monitoring**: Continuous (every 10 min)
- **Sensor placement**: Between inner cover and center of brood chamber
- **2020 thermoregulation**: Both colonies maintained high regulation; C2 better than C1
- **2021 thermoregulation**: Internal temperatures differed significantly between colonies

### Monitoring System Specifications
- **System**: BeeHive Monitoring System (Slovakia)
- **Data transfer**: GSM gateway with SIM phone card
- **Recording interval**: Every 10 minutes
- **External sensors**: Temperature (°C) and relative humidity (%)
- **Internal sensors**: Temperature (°C) and relative humidity (%)
- **Data storage**: Remote server

## Other Quantitative Data

### Study Area Characteristics
- **Melliferous plants**: Castanea sativa, Eucalyptus globulus, Rubus, heathers (Erica)

### Management Practices
- **Honey harvest**: September (both years)
- **Varroa treatment**: After honey harvest
- **Feeding**: October-November, sugar solution (1:1), 1 kg per week per hive if needed
- **Control methods**: Bait traps and electric harps (summer and autumn months)

### Statistical Analysis
- **Software**: IBM SPSS Statistics® for Windows version 22.0
- **Correlation method**: Spearman non-parametric correlation
- **Confidence interval**: 99% (α < 0.01)
- **Comparative analysis**: Independent samples t-Test (α < 0.05)

### Hornet Pressure Timeline
- **2020 pattern**: 
  - June-mid-August: Continuous increase
  - Mid-August: Decrease
  - Mid-September: Exponential increase to maximum
  - After September: Decrease
- **2021 pattern**: 
  - Similar to 2020 but peak shifted by ~1.5 months
  - Peak in October instead of September

### Colony Health Indicators
- **Key factor for survival**: Thermoregulatory capacity
- **Strongest colony**: Colony with highest thermoregulatory capacity
- **Survival factor**: Colony strength (even under high hornet pressure)

### Data Collection Details
- **Photograph analysis**: Manual counting of hornets in front of each colony
- **Environmental data collection**: Continuous monitoring throughout study period
- **Colony assessments**: Regular inspections by beekeeper

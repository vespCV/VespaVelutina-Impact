### Genetic Analysis
- **Samples genotyped**: 104 individuals from Great Britain
- **Genotyping error rate**: <3% (12% samples repeated)
- **COI sequences**: 4 submitted to GenBank (KY224070-KY224073)
- **COI sequence identity**: 96-99% to V. velutina sequences, 90% or lower to other Vespa species
- **Haplotype**: Single haplotype shared with all French samples and samples from Jiangsu and Zhejiang, China
- **Microsatellite loci**: 15 loci
- **Allele frequency comparison**: Significantly higher similarity to French than Asian samples (P<0.001 for all three UK locations)
- **STRUCTURE analysis**: K=2 (optimum), UK samples cluster with France
- **Diploid males**: All 19 anatomical males were diploid (heterozygous at 5-9 loci)
- **Haploid males**: Only present at egg stage
- **Queen mating**: Single queen mated with single drone (inferred from offspring genotypes)
- **Typical French queen mating**: 2.4-4.1 drones

### Additional Samples
- **Tetbury2**: Dead hornet in imported timber from Loire Valley, France (7 October)
- **Bath**: Dead hornet in camping equipment returned from Loire Valley (13 October)
- **Genetic relationship**: Tetbury2 consistent with Tetbury nest; Somerset and Bath had different alleles, suggesting independent introductions

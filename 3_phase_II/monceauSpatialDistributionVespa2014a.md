## All Other Quantitative Data

### Study Details
- **Study period**: August 2011
- **Study month**: August (period of dramatic increase of V. velutina predation)
- **Location**: Experimental apiary "La Grande Ferrade" (INRA, Villenave d'Ornon)
- **GPS coordinates**: N 44°47'27.05", W 0°34'38.35"
- **Environment**: 25-ha agricultural area, no insecticide treatment
- **Total apiary hives**: 9 beehives
- **Experimental hives**: 6 beehives (h1-h6) in single row of chestnut trees
- **Hive spacing**: 3 m apart
- **Hive appearance**: Identical grey paint, same shape and appearance

### Colony Size Assessment
- **Assessment method**: Number of frames fully covered by honeybees (maximum = 12)
- **Spring assessment** (March 23, 2011): h1: 6, h2: 7, h3: 9, h4: 10, h5: 9, h6: 10
- **Summer assessment** (July 22, 2011): h1: 11, h2: 10, h3: 11, h4: 10, h5: 10, h6: 5
- **Winter assessment** (December 1, 2011): h1: Dead, h2: 6, h3: 3, h4: 2, h5: 6, h6: 4
- **At experiment time**: 5 hives had 10-11 full frames, 1 hive (h6) had 5 frames

### Capture-Mark-Recapture Design
- **Total days**: 5 days (D1: 01/08/11, D2: 02/08/11, D3: 03/08/11, D8: 08/08/11, D9: 09/08/11)
- **D1 morning session**: 08:30 am - 12:00 noon
- **D1 afternoon session**: 02:00 pm - 05:00 pm
- **D2 and D3**: 09:00 am - 12:00 noon only
- **D8 and D9**: Morning captures only (100 and 60 hornets respectively) to count remaining marked individuals
- **Experimenters**: 3 experimenters in parallel (2 hives each)
- **Recapture exclusion**: Individuals recaptured <5 min after previous capture not counted

### Marking Technique
- **Paint type**: Weather-proof acrylic pencil brushes (Marabu GmbH and Co., Germany)
- **Marking location**: Dorsal part of hornets (to avoid spiracle clogging)
- **Colors used**: 14 different colors
- **Marking combinations**: 
  - (i) 1 on thorax
  - (ii) 1 on thorax + 1 on abdomen
  - (iii) 1 on thorax + 2 on abdomen
- **Maximum individuals monitorable**: 2,954 individuals
- **Durability test**: Checked on ca. 50 hornets for at least 3 weeks (Monceau et al. 2013c)

### Capture and Recapture Numbers
- **D1 morning**: 188 unmarked (all marked), 73 recaptured (125 total recaptures)
- **D1 afternoon**: 42 unmarked (all marked), 6 recaptured (6 total recaptures)
- **D2**: 64 unmarked (all marked), 13 recaptured (17 total recaptures)
- **D3**: 66 unmarked (all marked), 19 recaptured (26 total recaptures)
- **D8**: 95 unmarked (not marked)
- **D9**: 56 unmarked (not marked)
- **Total marked**: 360 hornets from D1 to D3
- **Total recaptured at least once**: 204 (56.67%)
- **D1 morning recapture rate**: 73.94% (73/188)
- **D1 afternoon recapture rate**: 59.52% (6/42)
- **D2 recapture rate**: 32.81% (13/64)
- **D3 recapture rate**: 28.79% (19/66)

### Lincoln-Petersen Index Estimates
- **D1-D2 estimate**: 354 hornets (95% CI: 328-380)
- **D2-D3 estimate**: 351 hornets (95% CI: 214-487)
- **Daily visiting estimate**: ~350 hornets visiting patch daily

### Daily Visits
- **Average visits per half-day**: 1.88 ± 0.23 (average range: 1.70-2.20)
- **Comparison between sessions**: Chi-square test χ² = 2.43, P = 0.49 (not significantly different)
- **Hornets recaptured ≥5 times**: 50 individuals (ca. 14% of overall marked sample)
- **Recapture range**: 5-15 times from D1 to D9

### Interval Between Consecutive Visits
- **Analysis group**: Hornets recaptured ≥5 times with ≥2 visits during same session
- **Linear effect**: F(1,175) = 4.54, P = 0.03 (significant decrease)
- **Quadratic effect**: F(1,175) = 1.28, P = 0.26 (not significant)
- **Individual differences**: Linear effect F(1,47) = 7.54, P < 0.01; Quadratic effect F(1,47) = 0.17, P = 0.68
- **Pattern**: Interval decreased linearly as function of successive recaptures

### Distribution Analysis
- **Individual-level analysis**: 6 of 50 hornets (12%) recaptured mainly on 1 hive
  - Individual 1: 87.50% on h1 (8 total recaptures)
  - Individual 2: 37.50% on h1, 62.50% on h3 (8 total)
  - Individual 3: 75.00% on h4 (8 total)
  - Individual 4: 83.33% on h2 (6 total)
  - Individual 5: 57.14% on h5, 21.43% on h3 (14 total)
  - Individual 6: 83.33% on h4 (6 total)
- **Fisher's exact test**: P < 0.05 for all 6 individuals
- **Binomial test**: P < 0.01 (recapture probability >1/6)
- **Other individuals**: Caught in front of all hives (Fisher's exact test P > 0.05)

### Global Distribution
- **First capture index**: I_d = 6.15, P < 0.05, n = 360 (aggregative)
- **Recaptures index**: I_d = 10.41, P < 0.05, n = 680 (aggregative)
- **Pooled index**: I_d = 630.02, P < 0.05, n = 1,040 (aggregative)
- **Distribution**: Not random, predominantly aggregated on h5
- **Hive distribution**: h5 most visited, h2/h3/h6 least visited, h1/h4 intermediate

### Marked vs Unmarked Variation
- **Overall difference**: ANCOVA F(1,8) = 1.35, P = 0.28 (not significant)
- **Course of survey**: F(1,8) = 3.31, P = 0.11 (not significant overall)
- **Marked individuals trend**: F(1,8) = 12.64, P < 0.01 (decreased over time)
- **Unmarked individuals trend**: Increased over time

### Additional Quantitative Findings
- **Maximum hornets at single hive**: Up to 20 hornets at same time in front of single hives at autumn (mid-September)
- **Estimated daily visits at peak**: Over 120 hornets visiting daily 6 hives at peak period
- **V. velutina nests**: 5 nests recorded within 1 km of experimental site
- **Foraging range reference**: V. mandarinia recorded at apiaries 1-2 km from nest, individuals can fly up to 8 km from nest

### Worker Longevity References
- **V. simillima**: Maximal longevity <30 days
- **V. tropica**: Workers may live >55 days
- **V. velutina**: Data not available

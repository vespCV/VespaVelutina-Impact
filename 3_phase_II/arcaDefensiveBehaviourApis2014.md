## All Other Quantitative Data

### Study Details
- **Study period**: August–October 2008 and August–September 2010
- **Study area**: 12,000 km² area in south-western France
- **Mean colonies per apiary**: 7.3 ± 2.4
- **Hornet population**: At maximum during study periods (Monceau et al. 2013a, 2013b)
- **V. velutina introduction**: First discovered in France in 2004
- **Spread**: Extended to Spain, Portugal, Italy
- **Estimated range 2012**: About 360,000 km² in Europe (Rome et al. 2013)
- **V. velutina colony size**: At least 3 times larger than European hornet Vespa crabro

### Experimental Design - Simulated Hornet Attacks
- **Test duration**: 5 minutes
- **Time steps**: 20 steps of 15 seconds each
- **T₀**: Natural conditions (no hornet)
- **T₁**: Hornet positioned 15 cm from hive entrance
- **T₂-T₂₀**: Hornet on hive's flight board
- **Hornet movement range**: 10 cm (fixed to 10 cm transparent nylon thread)
- **Camera distance**: 50 cm from hive's flight board
- **Camera type**: Sony digital camera
- **Video analysis**: Slow motion (1/5 of normal speed)
- **Analysis software**: LabWatcher (ViewPoint, France)
- **Activity measurement period**: First minute after hornet introduction
- **Image analysis software**: ImageJ
- **Screenshot times**: T₀ (before attack), T₁₀ (after 2 min 30 s), T₂₀ (after 5 min)
- **Balling classification**: Ordinal scale 0-4 (0 = no ball, 4 = >30 honeybees)

### Natural Observations
- **Behavioral pattern 1**: 42% of observed colonies - large number of honeybees gathered on flight board and vertical walls to form bee-carpet
- **Behavioral pattern 2**: 20% of observed colonies - bee-carpet with coordinated behavior, individuals clinging together and following hornet movements, frequent hissing, no shimmering
- **Behavioral pattern 3**: 38% of observed colonies - no coordinated behavior, honeybees dispersed on flight board and front wall
- **Propolis wall construction**: Only 1 colony (Saint-Sulpice-et-Cameyrac apiary)
- **Hornet contact with bee-carpet**: 6 instances observed at INRA apiary, only once did guard bees engulf and transport hornet inside hive
- **Dead hornets observed**: Never observed in front of hives
- **Hornet hovering distance**: About 15 cm from hive entrance
- **Hornet preference**: Returning foragers loaded with pollen (heavier, slower, easier to catch)

### Simulated Attack Results - Colony Activity
- **Activity drop**: Dramatic drop from T₀ (no hornet) to T₁ (hornet at entrance)
- **Predictors tested**: Apiary, colony, year (2008/2010), month, time sequencing exposure
- **All predictors significant**: P < 0.0001 for apiary and colony
- **Year effect**: P = 0.91 (not significant for activity)
- **Month effect**: P < 0.0001 (significant)
- **Time sequencing exposure**: P = 0.0001 (significant, negative impact)
- **Over-dispersion**: Activity model dispersion = 2.7

### Bee-Carpet Behavior
- **Colonies forming bee-carpet**: When hornet brought close to or placed on flight board
- **Defense duration**: Number of honeybees remained constant after 2-5 min (P > 0.01, Friedman's test)
- **Increase categories**:
  - 30-60% increase: 14 colonies
  - 60-80% increase: 17 colonies
  - >80% increase: 48 colonies
  - No increase: 16 colonies (5 formed bee-carpet at beginning, 11 had slightly increased defensive behavior and decreased foraging)
- **Total colonies analyzed**: 95 colonies
- **Over-dispersion**: Bee-carpet model dispersion = 55.7
- **All predictors significant**: P < 0.0001
- **Year effect**: P < 0.0001 (significant, negative impact)
- **Month effect**: P < 0.0001 (significant, negative impact)
- **Time sequencing exposure**: P = 0.0001 (significant, positive impact)
- **Long-term effect**: Number of guards declined with long-term predation (month and exposure duration)

### Balling Behavior - Outside Hive
- **Balling at T₁** (hornet 15 cm from entrance): Never observed
- **Balling when hornet on flight board**: 68 colonies (72%) exhibited this strategy
- **Ball size distribution**:
  - <10 bees: 31 colonies (33%)
  - 10-20 bees: 20 colonies (21%)
  - 20-30 bees: 15 colonies (16%)
  - >30 bees: 1 colony
- **Hornets killed**: 9 hornets (9.5%) killed by balling within 5 min
- **Hornets stung**: Only 2 of the 9 killed hornets were stung
- **Balling persistence**: When hornet moved away, only small group continued to form ball

### Balling Behavior - Inside Hive
- **Total colonies tested**: 7 colonies (B1-B7)
- **Total hornets tested**: 55 hornets
- **Total trials**: 55 trials
- **Hornet movement range inside hive**: 1-2 cm (limited by thread length)
- **Hornets killed**: 42 out of 55 (76.4%)
- **Hornets stung**: Only 1 of 42 dead hornets was stung
- **Killing success by duration**:
  - 15 min: 75% killed
  - 20 min: 60% killed
  - 30 min: 33.3% killed
  - 1 h: 100% killed

### Temperature Measurements
- **Probe type**: 2 mm micro-probes
- **Thermometer accuracy**: ±0.1°C (YCT RS-232 thermometer)
- **Probe 1 position**: Attached to hornet's thorax
- **Probe 2 position**: About 15 cm from hornet
- **Recording frequency**: Every 30 seconds
- **Mean maximum ball temperature**: 44.0 ± 0.9°C
- **Temperature progression**: Increased rapidly, stable for ~15 min after first 5-6 min, then decreased slightly but never returned to initial value
- **Colony temperature ranking** (highest to lowest average max temp): B2, B5, B3, B1, B4 and B6 (tied), B7
- **Colony B7 temperature**: Lowest maximum (40.1°C) but 100% killing success
- **Colony B3 temperature**: Average 43.9°C (ranked 3rd) but poor killing ability
- **Mean colony temperature** (probe 2): 23.8 ± 2.7°C
- **Mean environmental temperature**: 22.8 ± 2.0°C

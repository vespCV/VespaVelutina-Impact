# Quantitative Data: Pérez-Granados et al. 2024

## Template-Relevant Data

### Study Design
- **Country**: Spain (Girona province, NE Catalonia)
- **Start year**: 2018
- **End year**: 2020
- **Study duration**: 3 years
- **Number of apiaries**: 4 (different apiaries per year)
- **Number of beehives per apiary**: 
  - 2018: 40 hives per apiary (2 apiaries)
  - 2019-2020: 36 hives per apiary (1 apiary per year)
- **Locations**: Begudà, Santa Pau (2018); Batet de la Serra (2019); Falgons (2020)
- **Maximum distance between apiaries**: 15 km

### Electric Harp Specifications
- **2018 model**: 75 × 50 cm, wire separation 1.8 cm
- **2019-2020 model**: 86 × 74 cm, wire separation 2 cm
- **Number of harps**:
  - 2018: 2 harps per apiary
  - 2019-2020: 8 harps per apiary
- **Height above ground**: 20-30 cm
- **Power source**: Solar panel with 6V battery

### Monitoring Protocol
- **2018**: 
  - Begudà: 1 Oct - 30 Oct, 4 visits, 4 hours per visit, 32 working hours total
  - Santa Pau: 24 Sep - 23 Oct, 4 visits, 4 hours per visit, 32 working hours total
- **2019**: 
  - Batet de la Serra: 28 Aug - 21 Nov, 13 visits, 8 hours per visit, 832 working hours total
- **2020**: 
  - Falgons: 23 Sept - 24 Nov, 10 visits, 8 hours per visit, 640 working hours total

### Capture Data
- **Total insects trapped**: 3,331 individuals
- **V. velutina**: 90.5% of all insects trapped (range: 29.9-94.3% across years and locations)
- **Selectiveness variation**: Significant across years and locations

### Insect Categories
- **Domestic honey bees**: Second most frequently trapped category
- **European hornets (V. crabro)**: Counted separately
- **Native insects (excluding honeybees)**: Mean 1.2% of all insects trapped (range: 0-2.4%)

### Risk Assessment
- **Risk of damage to local entomofauna**: Very low in all surveyed areas and years
- **Native insects (excluding honeybees)**: Consistently low capture rates

## Other Quantitative Data

### Electric Harp System
- **Manufacturer**: Velzapper (local Spanish company)
- **System**: Distributed current to two channels, each with electric harps connected
- **Container design**: Mesh container allowing small insects (including honeybees) to exit, larger insects (hornets) accumulate in plastic trap
- **Improvement over previous models**: Container design vs. water receptacle

### Survey Timing
- **Survey period**: Maximum insolation period (10:00-15:00 CET)
- **2018**: Restricted to 4h duration
- **2019-2020**: Full survey day (ca. 8 working hours)

### Statistical Analysis
- **Selectiveness definition**: Proportion of Asian hornets trapped over total trapped insects × 100
- **Risk damage definition**: Proportion of native insects trapped over total trapped insects × 100

### Comparison with Previous Studies
- **Rojas-Nossa et al. 2022**: Found >90% V. velutina in electric harps
- **This study**: Confirms high selectiveness (90.5% overall, with variation)

### Management Implications
- **Environmental friendliness**: Electric harps suggested as useful environmentally-friendly method
- **Selectiveness**: High selectiveness maintained across time and locations
- **Bycatch**: Very low risk to native entomofauna

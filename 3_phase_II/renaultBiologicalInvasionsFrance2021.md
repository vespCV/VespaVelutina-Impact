# Quantitative Data: Renault et al. 2021

## Template-Relevant Data

### Study Design
- **Country**: France (Metropolitan France and French overseas territories)
- **Study period**: 1993-2018
- **Data source**: InvaCost database version 3.0
- **Total cost entries**: 1,118 entries (after filtering)
- **Annualised cost entries**: 1,583 entries
- **Number of species with cost data**: 98 invasive alien species

### Economic Costs
- **Total costs (1993-2018)**: US$ 11,535 million
- **Average annual costs**: US$ 444 million
- **Observed costs only (1993-2018)**: US$ 1,280 million
- **Average annual observed costs**: US$ 49.2 million
- **Highest cost period**: 2009-2012 (US$ 4,172 million total, US$ 1,043 million annually)
- **Cost entries per year (2009-2012)**: 168-283 entries per year

### Cost Types
- **Damage-Loss costs (2009-2012)**: US$ 732 million
- **Management costs (2009-2012)**: US$ 98 million
- **Damage:Management ratio**: 7-8:1
- **Costs before 2000**: Only 13 costs reported, all low values

### Impacted Sectors
- **Health sector**: US$ 324 million (cumulative)
- **Agriculture sector**: US$ 258 million (cumulative)
- **Authorities and Stakeholders**: US$ 230 million (cumulative)
- **Mixed sectors**: US$ 425 million (cumulative)

### Regional Distribution
- **Regions with lowest costs**: Grand Est, Ile de France, Hauts de France, Normandie (< 10 species, < US$5 million)
- **Regions with highest costs**:
  - Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes: US$ 238 million
  - La Réunion: US$ 137 million
- **Regions with >15 species and US$5-100 million**: Provence Alpes Côte d'Azur, Bretagne, Pays de la Loire, Nouvelle Aquitaine, New Caledonia

### Species with Costs
- **Total introduced/invasive species in France**: 2,750 species (as of September 24, 2020)
- **Species with cost data**: 98 species

- **Species with only observed costs**: 63 species

## Other Quantitative Data

### Data Collection
- **Initial InvaCost entries**: 28 entries from 16 English-written articles
- **French language search**: 26 papers found, 4 articles with monetary values (14 cost entries)
- **Expert contact data**: 1,106 cost entries from 39 French documents
- **Total documents analyzed**: 300 documents (1975-2021)
- **Documents selected for review**: 140 documents
- **Documents excluded**: 28 (unconnected to species), 19 (non-English), 30 (no accessible full text)

### Temporal Trends
- **Costs continuously increased**: 1993 to 2012
- **Costs decreased**: After 2012 (concomitant with decrease in reported cost estimates)
- **Time lag in reporting**: Indicated by decrease after 2012

### Costliest Species
- **Threshold for "costliest"**: Species with economic impacts exceeding US$ 1 million (1993-2018)
- **Species aggregated**: Impatiens spp., Ludwigia spp., Rattus spp., Reynoutria spp.

### Extrapolation Analysis
- **Total species in France**: 2,621 introduced and invasive species (after removing duplicates)
- **Species with cost data worldwide but not in France**: Used for extrapolation model
- **Regression model**: Linear regression of cost in France as function of cost worldwide (log-10 transformed)

### Database Information
- **InvaCost version**: 3.0
- **Total entries in database**: 9,823 cost entries
- **Currency standardization**: All costs standardized to 2017 equivalent US dollars
- **Method reliability**: Excluded entries with 'low' reliability

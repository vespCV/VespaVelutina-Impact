### Health Impacts
- **Defensive distance**: Within 3 metres from nest

### Control Methods
- **Trap selectivity**: V. velutina represents ~1% of trapped insects
- **Harmonic radar tracking**: Passive transponder ~15 mg
- **Radio tracking**: Active transmitter ~150-312 mg

### Geographic Spread
- **France**: Detected 2004, colonized 190,000 km² by 2010, 360,000 km² by 2012, most of country by 2017
- **Spain**: Detected 2010, spread to Galicia (2012), Catalonia (2012), Mallorca (2015, later eradicated)
- **Portugal**: Detected 2011, 62% of mainland colonized by 2020
- **Belgium**: First observation 2011, first nests 2016-2017
- **Italy**: First spotted 2012, first colonies 2013
- **Germany**: Started colonizing 2014, observed in Hamburg 2019
- **UK**: First observed 2016
- **Other countries**: Netherlands, Switzerland, Luxembourg, Ireland (single specimen)

### Asian Range
- **South Korea**: Discovered 2003, spread rate 10-20 km/year
- **Japan**: Spotted 2012 (Tsushima), 2015 (Kyushu), 2017 (Iki)

### Defensive Behaviour Data
- **A. cerana workers engaged**: 32.2 ± 3.2 workers
- **A. cerana core temperature**: 45.6 ± 0.1°C
- **A. mellifera workers engaged**: 22.7 ± 3.1 workers
- **A. mellifera core temperature**: 44.3 ± 0.2°C
- **Hawking success**: 3 times higher for A. mellifera than A. cerana

### Reproductive Interference
- **Japan**: 43% of native queen species inseminated by V. velutina males
- **Of these**: 28% had only V. velutina sperm

### Management Costs
- **Indirect costs increase**: 10% (beekeeping sector)

## Other Quantitative Data

### Research Topics Covered
- **Total topics**: 17 categories
- **Distribution**: Analyzed by continent and topic
- **Topics include**: Distribution, Species Distribution Models, impacts (biodiversity, ecosystem services, apiculture, human health, economy), monitoring, eradication, control, genetics, viruses/parasites, biology, taxonomy, reviews

### Distribution Data
- **Native range**: 12 color subspecies (4 mainland, 8 island)
- **France spread rate**: 78 km/year
- **Nest density (France, 2007-2014)**: 4.81 nests/km² (urban and non-urban), 10.23-12.26 nests/km² (urban only)
- **Spain spread rate**: Not specified
- **Portugal spread rate**: 37-45 km/year
- **Portugal nest density**: 5.4 ± 3.3 nests/km² (urban)
- **Italy spread rate**: 18.3 ± 3.3 km/year
- **Italy colonized area**: 205 km² (2013) to 930 km² (2015)

### Diet Analysis
- **Prey species identified**: 141 species
- **Orders**: 11 orders
- **Families**: 43 families of insects, 3 families of spiders, 4 families of vertebrates
- **Biomass consumption**: ~11.32 kg of insect biomass per colony per season
- **Hymenoptera**: 60.1% (honeybees: 38.1%, social wasps: 19.7%)
- **Diptera**: 29.9%
- **Habitat differences**:
  - Woodlands: Social wasps 28.3%, flies 31.5%, bees 33.4%
  - Urban areas: Social wasps 7.8%, flies 17.2%, bees 66.6%

### Predation Success
- **South Korea**: 80% overall, peaks up to 96% in September
- **Peak predation**: Around 9 hornets per hive
- **Predation period (France)**: July to October

### Economic Costs
- **France (2006-2015)**: €23 million
- **Projected costs (3 European countries)**: €29.5 million/year
- **Projected costs (2 Asian countries)**: €31.4 million/year

### Health Impacts
- **Defensive distance**: Within 3 metres from nest
- **Attack range**: Up to 300 m in some cases
- **Ocular lesions (France, until 2019)**: 29 cases (80% occupational exposure)

### Control Methods
- **Trap selectivity**: V. velutina represents ~1% of trapped insects
- **Harmonic radar tracking**: Passive transponder ~15 mg
- **Radio tracking**: Active transmitter ~150-312 mg

## Other Quantitative Data

### Morphological Characteristics
- **Queen length**: Up to 3.5 cm
- **Worker weight (wet)**: 188.8 ± 44.9 mg (June) to 386.4 ± 88.3 mg (November)
- **Queen weight**: 624 ± 15.2 mg (September) to 721.3 ± 73.2 mg (November)
- **Development stages**: 48.1 days total (13 days eggs, 15.8 days larvae, 19.3 days sealed brood)
- **Gynes maximum**: 191 ± 195.7 (November)
- **Colony size**: Mean 6,151 individuals (November), maximum 13,340 individuals
- **Combs**: Mean 8 combs (December)
- **Nest relocation**: ~70% of nests considered relocated colonies

### Geographic Spread
- **France**: Detected 2004, colonized 190,000 km² by 2010, 360,000 km² by 2012, most of country by 2017
- **Spain**: Detected 2010, spread to Galicia (2012), Catalonia (2012), Mallorca (2015, later eradicated)
- **Portugal**: Detected 2011, 62% of mainland colonized by 2020
- **Belgium**: First observation 2011, first nests 2016-2017
- **Italy**: First spotted 2012, first colonies 2013
- **Germany**: Started colonizing 2014, observed in Hamburg 2019
- **UK**: First observed 2016
- **Other countries**: Netherlands, Switzerland, Luxembourg, Ireland (single specimen)

### Asian Range
- **South Korea**: Discovered 2003, spread rate 10-20 km/year
- **Japan**: Spotted 2012 (Tsushima), 2015 (Kyushu), 2017 (Iki)

### Defensive Behaviour Data
- **A. cerana workers engaged**: 32.2 ± 3.2 workers
- **A. cerana core temperature**: 45.6 ± 0.1°C
- **A. mellifera workers engaged**: 22.7 ± 3.1 workers
- **A. mellifera core temperature**: 44.3 ± 0.2°C
- **Hawking success**: 3 times higher for A. mellifera than A. cerana

### Reproductive Interference
- **Japan**: 43% of native queen species inseminated by V. velutina males
- **Of these**: 28% had only V. velutina sperm

### Management Costs
- **Indirect costs increase**: 10% (beekeeping sector)

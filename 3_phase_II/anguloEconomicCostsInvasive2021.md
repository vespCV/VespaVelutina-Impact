## Other Quantitative Data

### Study Details
- **Data sources**: InvaCost database (v.3.0) + direct requests to regional/national authorities
- **Total cost entries**: >3,000 entries
- **Robust data only**: Excluding extrapolated, potential (not-incurred/expected), and low reliability costs
- **Total robust costs**: US$ 261 million (€ 232 million) from 1997 to 2022
- **Cost increase**: US$ 4 million/year before 2000 → US$ 15 million/year in recent years (€ 4 → € 13 million)
- **Species with reported costs**: 174 species
- **Cost types**: >90% management costs, damage costs found for only 2 species
- **Cost distribution**: 66% spent on post-invasion management (regional/inter-regional administrations)
- **Prevention vs management**: Contrary to international guidelines - more spent on management than prevention
- **Species not in regulations**: 36% of species with management costs not in national/European black lists

### Cost Categories
- **Management costs**: >90% of reported costs
- **Damage costs**: Only 2 out of 174 species
- **Prevention costs**: Not specified (likely minimal given focus on post-invasion management)

### Regional Reporting
- **Regional administrations**: Unequal reporting of costs
- **Neighboring regions**: Manage different groups of species

# Quantitative Data: O'Shea-Wheller et al. 2023

## Template-Relevant Data

### Study Design
- **Country**: Spain (Pontevedra province)
- **Start year**: 2021
- **Start month**: August
- **End month**: September
- **Study duration**: 40 days (9 August - 18 September 2021)
- **Number of sites**: 12 field sites (selected from 15 initial sites)
- **Number of colonies**: 36 Bombus terrestris terrestris colonies (3 per site)
- **Colony type**: Commercially-reared, each with queen and ~80 workers

### Hornet Monitoring
- **Traps per site**: 2 VespaCatch traps
- **Trap height**: 1.5 m from ground
- **Monitoring frequency**: Every 2 days
- **Total hornets caught**: 5,302 individuals (N=5302)
- **Hornet count data points**: 3,798 measurements

### Predation Observations
- **Type of observation**: Video (automated tracking)
- **Cameras per colony**: 2 (entrance camera + external camera)
- **Total cameras used**: 72 cameras
- **Video recording period**: 09:00-17:00 h
- **Video length**: 3-4 hours per recording
- **Total video data**: 648 hours from external cameras
- **Total trajectories tracked**: 135,983 individual trajectories from entrance cameras
- **Predation attempts observed**: 125 total attempts
- **Predation success rate**: 0% (100% failure rate)
- **Observation period for predation**: 27 days

### Environmental Conditions
- **Temperature monitoring**: Continuous (15 min intervals)
- **Relative humidity monitoring**: Continuous (15 min intervals)
- **Land cover analysis radius**: 1.5 km around each site
- **Land cover resolution**: 100 m²

### Colony Measurements
- **Weight measurements**: Every 2 days
- **Weight precision**: ±0.02g (PS 2100.R2.M.H balance)
- **Individual bee/hornet weighing**: 10 bees and 10 hornets per day from one site
- **Weight scale precision**: ±0.001g (HSW07 scales)
- **Total bee samples weighed**: 350 individuals
- **Total hornet samples weighed**: 350 individuals

## Other Quantitative Data

### Study Location
- **Coordinates**: 41.97903, -8.76852 (decimal degrees)
- **Location**: Near Tomiño, Pontevedra, Spain

### Colony Characteristics
- **Starting weight distribution**: Mean weight <±50g between sites
- **Acclimatization period**: 24-48 hours before sampling
- **Colony housing**: Waterproof polystyrene outer coatings

### Camera Specifications
- **Entrance camera height**: 110 mm above entrance tube
- **Entrance camera frame area**: ~0.011 m²
- **External camera distance**: 1 m from colony
- **External camera field of view**: ~1.5 m radius
- **Video normalization**: All videos normalized to 3 hours

### Tracking Software
- **Software**: Modified Camlytics (release v.2.2.5)
- **Tracking method**: AI-assisted tracking

### Environmental Variables
- **Data logger**: TinytagPlus2-TGP-4500
- **Recording interval**: 15 minutes
- **Land cover dataset**: CORINE 2018
- **GIS software**: ArcGIS Pro (release v.3.0)

### Predation Behavior Details
- **Predation attempt sequence**: Hornet pursues → grabs bee in flight → bee drops to ground → hornet loses grip → bee escapes
- **Defensive posture**: Bees fall on back with legs and sting raised
- **Weight ratio**: Worker weight ratio did not influence predation (F₁,₂₀₄ = 0.003, P=0.959)
- **Hornet counts**: Did not influence predation attempt frequency (F₁,₈₃ = 1.237, P=0.269)

### Foraging Activity
- **Peak foraging**: Mid-August
- **Foraging frequency**: Positively associated with hornet presence
- **Foraging response to hornets**: Little discernible response (no substantial foraging inhibition)

### Comparison with Honey Bees
- **Honey bee predation success rate**: ~30% (from previous work)
- **Bombus predation success rate**: 0% (this study)

### Dietary Analysis Context
- **Previous dietary analysis sample size**: 2,515 prey items
- **Bombus records in previous analysis**: 1 record (B. lapidarius)

### Pathogen Analysis
- **Colonies screened**: 10 colonies (subset)
- **Pathogen screening method**: RT-qPCR
- **Disease observed**: Dysentery

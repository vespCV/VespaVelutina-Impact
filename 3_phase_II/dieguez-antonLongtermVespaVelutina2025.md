# Quantitative Data: Diéguez-Antón et al. 2025

## Template-Relevant Data

### Study Design
- **Country**: Spain (Galicia, NW Spain)
- **Start year**: 2021
- **End year**: 2022
- **Study period**: February 2021 - November 2022
- **Number of apiaries**: 3 apiaries
- **Number of colonies per apiary**: Variable (4-20 colonies)
- **Monitored colonies**: 2 hives per apiary (6 total)

### Apiary Locations
- **Apiary 1**: A Coruña province, 260 m elevation, southwest facing
- **Apiary 2**: A Coruña province (coastal), 4 m elevation, southwest facing
- **Apiary 3**: Pontevedra province, 60 m elevation, southeast facing
- **First V. velutina nest detection**: 2015 (all three areas)

### Monitoring Protocol
- **Camera type**: APEMAN 20MP 1080P Hunting Camera
- **Camera distance**: 1 m from hives
- **Photo window area**: 200 × 150 cm²
- **Photo frequency**: Every hour from 6:00 am to 11:00 pm
- **Photos per day**: 17 photos
- **Total photos per year per apiary**: ~6,205 photos

### Environmental Conditions
- **Monitoring system**: Beehive Monitoring System
- **Data transmission**: GSM (Global System for Mobile)
- **Recording frequency**: Every hour
- **Parameters recorded**: External temperature (°C), external relative humidity (%)

### Predation Pressure Duration
- **Apiary 1**: 8 months (2021), 6 months (2022)
- **Apiary 2**: 5 months (both years)
- **Apiary 3**: 11 months (both years)
- **Overall period**: April to February (following year)

### Hornet Counts
- **Total hornets (Apiary 1)**: 2,860 (1,172 in 2021, 1,688 in 2022)
- **Total hornets (Apiary 2)**: 2,074 (742 in 2021, 1,332 in 2022)
- **Total hornets (Apiary 3)**: 6,472 (3,373 in 2021, 3,099 in 2022)
- **Peak months**:
  - Apiary 1: July 2021 (839), October 2022 (494)
  - Apiary 2: August (461 in 2021, 555 in 2022)
  - Apiary 3: July (1,219 in 2021, 985 in 2022)

### Risk Thresholds
- **Period 1**: 1-4 hornets (arrival to low pressure)
- **Period 2**: 5-9 hornets (moderate pressure)
- **Period 3**: ≥10 hornets (high pressure)
- **Risk threshold**: ≥5 hornets poses risk to colony survival

### Temperature Analysis
- **Optimal temperature range**: 17-26 °C (most suitable for observing higher number of hornets)
- **Statistical analysis**: Spearman non-parametric correlation (99% confidence interval, p < 0.01)
- **Linear regression**: Relationship between external temperature and number of hornets

### Survival Analysis
- **Method**: Kaplan-Meier survival rates
- **Risk variables**: 
  - ≥5 hornets (minimum risk)
  - ≥10 hornets (high risk)
- **Statistical test**: Log-rank test

### High Pressure Events
- **Apiary 1 (October 2021)**: 31 events with >5 hornets, 5 days with >10 hornets
- **Apiary 2 (August 2021)**: 20 days with ≥5 hornets, up to 37 times
- **Apiary 2 (2022)**: Increased pressure compared to 2021

## Other Quantitative Data

### First Hornet Observations
- **Apiary 1**: May 13, 2021 and May 24, 2022
- **Apiary 2**: May 31, 2021 and June 12, 2022
- **Apiary 3**: Not specified in abstract

### Temporal Patterns
- **Activity period**: 16 hours per day (6:00 am - 11:00 pm)
- **Peak activity**: July-October (varies by apiary and year)
- **Pressure increase**: From July onwards in most cases

### Colony Observations
- **Honeybee presence**: Estimated during July and September (months of maximum hornet activity)
- **Foraging paralysis**: Observed when hornet pressure is high

### Statistical Methods
- **Software**: IBM SPSS Statistics version 22.0
- **Correlation analysis**: Spearman non-parametric
- **Confidence intervals**: 95% for mean hornet pressure per hour
- **Significance level**: 99% confidence interval (p < 0.01)

### Environmental Context
- **Climate**: Atlantic climate (Galicia)
- **Melliferous plants**: Castanea sativa, Eucalyptus globulus, Rubus, Erica, fruit trees (Malus, Pyrus, Prunus)
- **V. velutina establishment**: Since 2012 in Galicia
- **Nest density**: ~30,000 nests recorded each year since 2018

### Management Recommendations
- **Artificial feeding**: Recommended when foraging is stopped
- **Control methods**: Essential for colony survival
- **Colony strength**: Key factor for survival
